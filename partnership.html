Partnership

Coded so far:
Feb 2017

A partnership is an association of two or more persons to be the co-owners of a business for profit. 0

In determining whether a partnership exists, the primary question is "whether the parties intentionally acted as co-owners of a business for profit, and not on whether they consciously intended to create the legal relationship of 'partner-ship.'" 1

The burden of proof to show a partnership is on the party alleging the partnership. 2 3

Generally, the party alleging the partnership is required to prove that a partnership exists by a preponderance of the evidence. 4

Where the alleged partners are relatives the party alleging the partnership is required to prove the existence of the partnership by clear and convincing evidence. 2 3 5

MCL 449.7 lays out specific rules for determining the existence of partnership. 6

The receipt by a person of a share of the profits of a business is prima facie evidence that he is a partner in the business 7

MCL 449.7(4) lays out several exceptions to the "prima facie evidence" rule. It states that no inference of partnership is drawn if the profits were received if in payment "[a]s a debt by installments or otherwise,". 8
