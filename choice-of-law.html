In tort cases, Michigan courts use a choice-of-law analysis called ‘interest analysis’ to determine which state's law governs a suit where more than one state’s law may be implicated. Under this analysis, Michigan courts will apply Michigan law unless a ‘rational reason’ to do otherwise exists. 0 1

In performing the interest analysis, the court first examines whether any foreign state has an interest in having its law apply. 0

If no state has an interest in its law applying, the presumption that Michigan law will apply cannot be overcome. If a foreign state does have an interest in having its law applied, the court uses a “balancing approach” to “determine if Michigan’s interests mandate that Michigan law be applied, despite the foreign interests 0

A state has an interest in having its law applied if one of the parties is a citizen of the state where the wrong occurred 2
